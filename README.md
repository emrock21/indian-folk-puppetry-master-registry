# Indian Folk Puppetry Master Registry

This registry documents the regional puppetry traditions of India, including their lineages, materials, mechanisms, performance traits, and cultural uniqueness.

## Purpose

To preserve India's diverse puppetry heritage on-chain in a permanent, verifiable, and censorship-resistant format.

Each entry records:

- region  
- lineage or workshop  
- style name  
- materials  
- mechanism (string, rod, glove, shadow)  
- performance traits  
- cultural uniqueness  

## Why this registry matters

India has one of the richest puppetry traditions in the world, including:

- Kathputli (Rajasthan)  
- Bommalattam (Tamil Nadu)  
- Togalu Gombeyaata (Karnataka)  
- Kundhei (Odisha)  
- Putul Nach (West Bengal)  
- Pavakathakali (Kerala)  
- Yampuri (Bihar)  
- Benir Putul (Assam)  

Each tradition has distinct materials, mechanisms, and performance aesthetics.
